Who we are:


We formed Blackout Booking Management to get you on stage so you can focus on your art.

We are, of course, musicians ourselves, and if you've been in the music scene for long in Columbia, SC, you've probably seen us play in one band or another.

I'm Hannah, and I'm currently in three.

And what we've surely all observed, and if you're here you yourself probably feel, is that often, being driven to make art and having an interest in logistics, business, networking, scheduling, negotiating, etc., do not always naturally complement each other.

I know plenty of artists myself who are incredibly talented but not necessarily the greatest at pulling it all together.

And that's what we're here for: to help you pull it all together, so you can stay focused on the side of this that is most inspiring: being creative, making art, playing music.

Our own reasons for starting Blackout are because, if one must work, it's a lot more satisfying to do something that's integral to what we love already.

But on a deeper level, it's because we want powerful artistic personalities on stages, where they belong, lighting people up -- the world needs real art, and the music we get to experience should not be limited to that made only by those with a flair for the managerial.



Blackout exists to bridge that gap. Your job is to bring art into the world. Ours is to make sure people hear it.

Values:


Blackout Booking Management is woman-owned and operated, by members of the LGBT community, and as such, we are committed as an organization to fostering an art scene that affirms, embraces, and promotes the expression of people of all genders, sexualities, races -- our interest is in working with artists who are grounded in these values.

What are we looking to do?


Blackout operates on a percentage basis: we handle the legwork of putting together the best possible events to showcase your work, and the fullness of the room leaves us all in the same boat.
